Welcome to Plurality For Singlets! Let's get started with the obvious questions:

What's Plurality?

Plurality is to have more than one individual sharing a body. These individuals are often referred to as headmates, system members, or alters. These individuals from a system. Members in the system may front individually or in groups. Fronting is the term for being "in control" of the body.

NOTE: There are a lot of negative connotations surrounding the term alters, so unless you have been given express permission to refer to system members as such, it's best avoided.

What's a Singlet?

A singlet is, quite simply put, someone who isn't plural. The term is by no stretch an insult, and is simply used to differentiate systems from non-systems, so please don't take it negatively!

Common Courtesy

One of the most common hurdles that systems face is a bombardment of "I just don't know how to talk to you." Below are some handy do's and don'ts:

DO

  • Use collective pronouns and system names: Many systems have collective pronouns and system names that can be used to refer to the system as a whole, especially if you aren't sure who is fronting. For example, our system, The Flock, uses it/its collectively, even though individual members have their own pronouns.
  • Make an effort to get to know members individually: System members are individuals, and will generally have different interests and preferences.

DON'T

  • Ask when a certain member will be fronting: Generally speaking, we don't know! Some members can be brought to front voluntarily, but this often puts stress on the system and can cause headaches or confusion.
  • Ask why a bot is talking: Many plural systems use a bot called PluralKit to talk on Discord. Due to the nature of the program, their communications display the bot tag, but they themselves are not bots - and we get very, very tired of the jokes.
